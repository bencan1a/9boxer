## Grid Layout

The 9-box grid is a 3Ã—3 matrix that plots employees based on two dimensions:

- **X-axis (horizontal)**: **Performance** - Current performance in their role
- **Y-axis (vertical)**: **Potential** - Future growth capacity and leadership capability

Each axis has three levels: **Low**, **Medium**, and **High**, creating 9 distinct positions.

### Visual Grid Structure

```
                 LOW               MEDIUM            HIGH
              PERFORMANCE        PERFORMANCE      PERFORMANCE

HIGH          Enigma/              High            Stars
POTENTIAL     Question Mark       Potential       (Top Talent)
              Position 7          Position 8       Position 9

MEDIUM        Under-              Core             High
POTENTIAL     Performer           Performer        Performer
              Position 4          Position 5       Position 6

LOW           Too New/            Solid            Strong
POTENTIAL     Problem             Performer        Performer
              Position 1          Position 2       Position 3
```

### Axis Definitions

**Performance (X-axis):**

- **Low** - Below expectations, needs improvement
- **Medium** - Meets expectations, solid contribution
- **High** - Exceeds expectations, outstanding performance

**Potential (Y-axis):**

- **Low** - Limited growth capacity, best suited to current role
- **Medium** - Moderate growth potential, can advance with development
- **High** - High growth capacity, future leader potential

## The 9 Grid Positions

> ðŸ“‹ **Real-World Scenario**
>
> James is reviewing his leadership team and sees 8 out of 15 employees in Position 9 (Stars). He refers to the grid position definitions and realizes that 53% in the top box indicates grade inflation. Using the position definitions, he recalibrates by asking "Who truly has High Potential for the next level?" and moves 4 employees to Position 6 (High Performers without high potential), creating a more realistic distribution.

Each position on the grid has a specific meaning and strategic implications. Here's a detailed breakdown of all 9 positions:

---

### Position 9: Stars [High Performance, High Potential]

**Top-right corner - Your most valuable talent**

**Characteristics:**

- Top performers with high future leadership potential
- Exceed expectations consistently
- Ready for bigger roles and responsibilities
- Demonstrate strong leadership qualities

**Strategic Actions:**

- **Highest retention priority** - Keep them engaged and challenged
- **Succession planning candidates** - Identify them for key leadership roles
- **Stretch assignments** - Give them growth opportunities
- **Competitive compensation** - Ensure they're rewarded appropriately

**Typical Population:** 10-15% of workforce

!!! success "Stars"
    These are your future leaders. Invest heavily in their development and retention.

---

### Position 8: High Potential [Medium Performance, High Potential]

**Top-middle - Future leaders in development**

**Characteristics:**

- High potential but not yet performing at the highest level
- May be new to role or need more experience
- Show strong leadership capability
- On an upward trajectory

**Strategic Actions:**

- **Development focus** - Provide training, mentoring, coaching
- **Fast-track opportunities** - Accelerate their growth with challenging projects
- **Experience building** - Give them time and exposure to learn
- **Monitor progress** - Track their movement toward Position 9

**Typical Population:** 15-20% of workforce

!!! tip "High Potential"
    These employees need development and experience. With the right investment, they'll become your Stars.

---

### Position 7: Enigma / Question Mark [Low Performance, High Potential]

**Top-left - High potential but not yet performing**

**Characteristics:**

- High potential but currently underperforming
- May be too new to rate accurately
- Could be struggling with role fit or support
- Require close monitoring

**Strategic Actions:**

- **Investigate causes** - Understand why they're not performing
- **Provide support** - Ensure they have resources, training, and mentorship
- **Consider role fit** - May be in the wrong position
- **Set clear expectations** - Define performance goals and timelines
- **Monitor closely** - Decide if they can improve or need a change

**Typical Population:** 5-10% of workforce

!!! warning "Enigma"
    These employees have potential but aren't delivering. Find out why and provide support, or consider whether they're in the right role.

---

### Position 6: High Performer [High Performance, Medium Potential]

**Middle-right - Excellent in current role**

**Characteristics:**

- Excellent performance in current role
- May not want or be ready for advancement
- Critical contributors and subject matter experts
- Stable, reliable, high output

**Strategic Actions:**

- **Retain and reward** - Recognize their contributions with compensation and perks
- **Leverage expertise** - Use them as mentors, trainers, or specialists
- **Lateral growth** - Offer new challenges within current level
- **Avoid forced promotion** - Not everyone wants or should be promoted

**Typical Population:** 15-20% of workforce

!!! note "High Performer"
    These are your critical individual contributors. Don't assume they need to be promotedâ€”they're valuable where they are.

---

### Position 5: Core Performer [Medium Performance, Medium Potential]

**Center - The backbone of your organization**

> ðŸ“‹ **Real-World Scenario**
>
> Marcus runs a Donut Mode exercise and discovers that 30 of his 45 "Core Talent" employees are actually mis-placed. 12 are high-performers who should be in Position 6 or 8, while 8 are really just meeting minimums and belong in Position 2. The remaining 25 truly are solid, reliable Core Talent. This validates his center box and helps him recalibrate ratings accurately.

**Characteristics:**

- Meet expectations consistently
- Solid, reliable employees
- Backbone of the organization
- Stable contributors

**Strategic Actions:**

- **Recognize contributions** - Acknowledge their steady performance
- **Provide development** - Offer opportunities to grow into Position 6 or 8
- **Monitor carefully** - Use [Donut Mode](donut-mode.md) to validate placements
- **Avoid complacency** - Ensure they're not stagnating

**Typical Population:** 50-60% of workforce

!!! info "Core Performer"
    This is typically your largest group. These employees keep your organization running smoothly. Validate placements with Donut Mode to ensure accuracy.

---

### Position 4: Under-Performer [Low Performance, Medium Potential]

**Middle-left - Needs improvement**

**Characteristics:**

- Not meeting performance expectations
- Has potential but not currently delivering
- May need training, support, or clearer expectations

**Strategic Actions:**

- **Performance improvement plan** - Set clear goals and timelines
- **Provide support** - Training, coaching, resources
- **Monitor progress** - Track improvement over time
- **Consider role fit** - May be in wrong position
- **Take action** - If no improvement, consider reassignment or exit

**Typical Population:** 5-10% of workforce

!!! warning "Under-Performer"
    These employees need improvement. Provide support and clear expectations, but be prepared to take action if they don't improve.

---

### Position 3: Strong Performer [High Performance, Low Potential]

**Bottom-right - Excel in current role, limited advancement**

**Characteristics:**

- Excel in current role with limited desire or capacity for advancement
- Valuable individual contributors
- Stable, reliable, experienced
- May prefer to stay in current role

**Strategic Actions:**

- **Retain and reward** - Recognize their contributions
- **Leverage expertise** - Use as mentors or specialists
- **Lateral opportunities** - Offer variety within current level
- **Respect preferences** - Not everyone wants to advance

**Typical Population:** 10-15% of workforce

!!! note "Strong Performer"
    These employees are excellent at what they do. Respect their choice to remain in their current role and reward them accordingly.

---

### Position 2: Solid Performer [Medium Performance, Low Potential]

**Bottom-middle - Reliable workers, steady contributors**

**Characteristics:**

- Reliable workers who meet minimum standards
- Steady contributors with limited growth trajectory
- Prefer stability over advancement

**Strategic Actions:**

- **Recognize contributions** - Acknowledge their reliability
- **Maintain standards** - Ensure they continue to meet expectations
- **Provide stability** - Offer consistency and clear expectations
- **Monitor for decline** - Ensure they don't slip to Position 1

**Typical Population:** 10-15% of workforce

!!! info "Solid Performer"
    These employees provide stability and consistency. Ensure they remain engaged and don't decline.

---

### Position 1: Too New / Problem [Low Performance, Low Potential]

**Bottom-left - Concern area**

**Characteristics:**

- Too new to rate accurately, or
- Performance improvement needed with limited potential
- May have fit issues or capability gaps

**Strategic Actions:**

- **For new hires**: Give time to ramp up, provide onboarding support
- **For existing employees**: Performance improvement plan or exit discussion
- **Investigate root causes**: Lack of fit, support, training, or capability
- **Take action**: Improvement, reassignment, or exit

**Typical Population:** 5-10% of workforce

!!! danger "Too New / Problem"
    For new employees, this is temporary. For existing employees, this requires urgent attention and action.

## Color Coding

9Boxer uses visual color coding to help you quickly identify different areas of the grid:

- **Green boxes** (top row) - High potential employees - your future leaders
- **Yellow/Amber boxes** (middle row) - Medium potential employees - solid contributors
- **Orange boxes** (bottom row) - Lower potential or new hires - stable performers or concerns

**Employee Tile Colors:**

- **Blue tiles** - Default appearance for all employees
- **Orange left border** - Employee has been moved (modified in current session)
- **Purple border + ghostly** - Employee has been placed in Donut Mode

## Interpreting Grid Distribution

Understanding your grid distribution helps identify organizational strengths and risks.

### Ideal Distribution (Rough Guideline)

- **10-15% in Stars** (Position 9) - Top talent pipeline
- **15-20% in High Potential** (Positions 7, 8) - Future leaders
- **50-60% in Core/Solid Performers** (Positions 2, 5, 6) - Stable contributors
- **10-20% in Lower Boxes** (Positions 1, 3, 4) - Improvement or stability focus

!!! note "Guidelines, Not Rules"
    These percentages are rough guidelines. Your ideal distribution may vary based on industry, growth stage, and organizational needs.

### Red Flags to Watch For

**Too many in bottom-left (Position 1):**

- Performance issues or poor hiring
- Need for performance management or exits

**Too few in top rows (Positions 7, 8, 9):**

- Succession planning risk
- Limited leadership pipeline
- Retention concerns

**Heavily skewed to one side:**

- Calibration needed across managers
- Potential rating inconsistency

**Very uneven distribution:**

- Possible rating bias
- Lack of differentiation
- Over-reliance on "safe" middle ratings

## Using the Grid Strategically

### Talent Review Sessions

- Review each box systematically
- Validate placements using [Donut Mode](donut-mode.md)
- Discuss development plans for high potentials
- Address under-performers with action plans

### Succession Planning

- Identify Stars and High Potentials for key roles
- Develop succession plans for critical positions
- Track movement over time

### Performance Management

- Use grid to guide compensation decisions
- Identify employees needing development or action plans
- Ensure fair distribution across teams

### Retention Priorities

- Focus retention efforts on Stars and High Potentials
- Recognize and reward High Performers
- Address concerns in Position 1 proactively
